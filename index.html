🧠 Sensor Topology (10-20 System)

The 10-20 system is a standardized method for electrode placement. "10" and "20" refer to the percentages of the distance between Nasion (bridge of nose), Inion (bump at back of skull), and Pre-auricular points (front of the ears).

🟦 Frontal Lobe
Sensors: AF3, F7, F3, FC5, FC6, F4, F8, AF4
Function: Associated with the Dorsolateral Prefrontal Cortex (DLPFC). Responsible for executive functions, decision making, and attention.
🟧 Temporal Lobe
Sensors: T7, T8 (Also known as T3, T4)
Function: Located above the Superior Temporal Gyrus. Involved in auditory processing and language comprehension.
🟪 Parietal Lobe
Sensors: P7, P8
Function: Linked to the Inferior Parietal Lobe. Plays a key role in sensory integration and visuospatial attention.
🟥 Occipital Lobe
Sensors: O1, O2
Function: Visual processing center. This is the primary source of Alpha waves (Berger Effect) which increase significantly when eyes are closed.
Refs:
1. Morley et al., "10-20 system EEG Placement", ERS (2016).
2. Herwig et al., "Using the international 10-20 EEG system for positioning of transcranial magnetic stimulation.", Brain Topography. (2003).
